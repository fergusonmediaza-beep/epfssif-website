Why Donate Here

Why Your Donation Belongs Here

South Africa's structural unemployment crisis is a multi-generational challenge. Conventional philanthropy has failed because capital is deployed once, consumed once, and gone.

Traditional Philanthropy: The Problem

  • You write a cheque. You receive a receipt. You never know what it created.
  • No job count, duration, or proof for your report.
  • Capital is consumed once — the impact stops the moment funding ends.
  • Annual CSI committees ask: "What did last year's donation actually do?"

EPF SSIF 1: The Solution

  • Every contribution tracks to a named asset, a named entrepreneur, a verified job.
  • Audited impact certificate: jobs created, duration, skills, and enterprise revenue reported quarterly.
  • Covenant reinvestment engine: your capital keeps creating jobs long after you donate.
  • Structured for Integrated Reporting — a legacy your stakeholders can read.

Tax Efficiency & Structuring

How Section 18A Works for You

Contributions via the registered PBO qualify for Section 18A tax deductibility under the South African Income Tax Act — reducing the net cost of your philanthropy and increasing its leverage.

1
You Contribute

Make a donation of any amount from R1 000 to the registered PBO. No upper limit on tax-deductible contributions (subject to income limits).

2
Certificate Issued Immediately

Section 18A tax deduction certificate issued upon receipt of funds. Valid for submission with your SARS annual tax return.

3
Deduction Against Taxable Income

Your contribution is deductible against your taxable income, reducing your effective tax liability.

4
Structured for Reporting

Audited impact receipts structured for use in your family office's integrated or sustainability report.
